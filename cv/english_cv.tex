\section{Conference Papers}

\begin{tabularx}{\linewidth}{@{}X r@{}}
\textbf{$\bullet$ Should I stay calm:
How does the combination of visual and textual frames influence expressions of anger in short video comment sections?} & \textit{AEJMC 2026} \\
\end{tabularx}
\quad \textit{Li, Zihao.*} \hfill [\href{https://some-link.com}{\faGlobe}]
\begin{itemize}[nosep, leftmargin=2em, itemsep=3pt, label=$\circ$]
\item This study proposes an analytical method for measuring visual and textual frames in dynamic videos, which was subsequently tested on 439 short videos on Douyin related to the issue of drug addiction in China. Machine learning was then used to identify expressions of anger on a large scale in the comment sections of short video platforms covering this topic, which were used as the dependent variable in a regression analysis.
\item The results indicate that visual frames take precedence in emotional framing. Different combinations of visual and textual frames correspond significantly to varying degrees of anger expression, and consistency between them influences anger expression in comment sections. Multimodal combinations better explain emotional differences in comment sections than single-modal factors.
\end{itemize}
