  - "**Research Question:** Moving from a focus on the image production capabilities of AI and users' ability to distinguish AI images, this study asks: How does surrealism emerge in these AI-generated images? What types of surrealism exist? Through what visual elements are they expressed? And how do they reflect the visual logic of the generative AI era?"
  - "**Literature Logic:** The gap in research on AI-generated images — Surrealism — Algorithmic surrealism — Viewing surrealism as a meaningful content feature in algorithmically mediated visual communication — Proposing questions on how to describe and classify such content, and how to analyze it using traditional supervised/unsupervised approaches alongside large language model-based approaches."
  - "**Theoretical Framework:** Surrealism drawn from art theory (breaking rational order through imagination, dreams, and the subconscious). Does this article have a strict theoretical framework? Or is it perhaps using a theoretical concept to expand the interpretation of a phenomenon?"
  - "**Method:** The authors collected 28,290 images from 47 AI image creator accounts on Instagram, and retained 26,771 photorealistic AI-generated images after manual cleaning. The study used a mixed-methods framework assisted by large language models. First, a surrealism codebook with three categories (physical surrealism, behavioral surrealism, and contextual surrealism) was established through manual annotation and qualitative analysis. GPT-4o was then used to classify and annotate the large-scale sample. After that, GPT-4o was used to generate text summaries for the images, which were used for LDA topic modeling and topic network analysis to identify recurring visual elements and their co-occurrence relationships in surreal images."
  - "**Key Findings:** The study found that surrealism can serve as a prominent feature of realistic AI images, with about 66.9% of the sample containing surrealism, among which physical surrealism is the most common. Further analysis shows that these images often present mixed surrealism, concentrated in numerous visual elements. In the discussion, the authors argue that while algorithmic surrealism expands visual imagination, it may also lead to visual homogenization, reproduction of stereotypes, aestheticization of technical flaws, and political misinformation, which are relatively familiar concerns."
